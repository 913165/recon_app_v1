RRN (Retrieval Reference Number) and UTR (Unique Transaction Reference) are both unique 12-to-22 digit codes used to track financial transactions, but they differ in usage. RRN is primarily used for UPI and card payments to resolve disputes, while UTR is used for NEFT, RTGS, and IMPS bank transfers. Both help track payment status. [1, 2, 3, 4, 5]  
Key Differences Between RRN and UTR 

• Usage: RRN (12-digit) is typically generated for UPI and card transactions. UTR is used for NEFT, RTGS, and IMPS. 
• Purpose: Both allow banks to track and reconcile transactions; however, RRN is essential for disputes regarding failed or reversed digital payments. 
• Context: UTR acts as a formal bank-to-bank reference (often with a date-coded format), whereas RRN is often used in the backend of the UPI ecosystem. 
• Where to find: Both are found in transaction receipts, SMS notifications, or the UPI app's transaction history. [1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 7, 8, 9]  

What is RRN?RRN stands for Retrieval Reference Number, a 12-digit identifier generated by the bank/payment network when a UPI or card payment is initiated. It is critical for tracking funds, confirming payments, and resolving disputes. [2, 3, 5, 10]  
What is UTR?UTR stands for Unique Transaction Reference, a distinct identifier assigned to every bank transaction—particularly NEFT and RTGS—to distinguish it from others. [11]  
Do they overlap?In some scenarios, they are used interchangeably; for instance, some UPI apps label the 12-digit UPI reference number as a UTR number, but technically it functions as an RRN for that UPI ecosystem. [8, 9]
